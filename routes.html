Slovenia



Off to Slovenia

Slovenia is a hidden gem for adventurous travelers seeking majestic mountains, crystal-clear rivers, charming towns, and peaceful lakes. Whether you’re winding through the Julian Alps or relaxing by Lake Bohinj, this compact country has everything you need for a perfect camper van trip. From thrilling outdoor adventures to pure relaxation in nature, Slovenia has it all. So, pack up your camper, start the engine, and get ready to explore one of Europe’s best-kept secrets!

Overnight Stays in Slovenia

Camping in Slovenia is easy and affordable. You'll find a wide range of well maintained campsites, often in stunning natural settings. Wild camping is officially prohibited and can lead to fines, so stick to designated motorhome areas and official campsites, many of which are in spectacular locations.

Useful Information for Your Trip

Currency: Euro
Country Code: +386
Emergency Number: 112
Dutch Embassy: Poljanski nasip 6, 1000 Ljubljana
Tel: +386 1 420 1480

Toll Roads and Vignette

To use highways and expressways in Slovenia, you’ll need a toll vignette (for vehicles under 3.5 tons). You can buy it at border crossings, gas stations, or online before your trip. Be sure to attach it to your windshield before entering any toll road to avoid fines.

Fuel and Charging Stations

Diesel (Dizel) is widely available, even in rural areas. LPG stations are less frequent, so plan ahead if you use it. Electric vehicles can rely on a growing network of charging points, especially near cities and tourist zones.

Driving in Slovenia

Driving in Slovenia is safe and scenic. Mountain roads can be steep and winding, take your time and enjoy the views. In winter, snow chains are mandatory when road signs indicate.
Speed limits for campervans (≤ 3.5 tons):
- Urban areas: 50 km/h
- Rural roads: 90 km/h
- Highways: 130 km/h
Just like at home, the legal alcohol limit is 0.5‰. So enjoy Slovenia’s excellent wines but drink responsibly.

Bringing Pets

Traveling with pets in the EU requires:
- A European pet passport
- A microchip
- A rabies vaccination
Pets are welcome at most campsites in Slovenia, but check ahead to be sure.

Sample Itinerary: 10 Days Across Slovenia

Day 1-2: Kranjska Gora and Triglav National Park
Start in the alpine village of Kranjska Gora. Visit Lake Jasna and drive the spectacular Vršič Pass. Go hiking or simply unwind in nature.

Day 3-4: Bovec and the Soča Valley
Head south to the Soča Valley, a paradise for outdoor lovers. Try rafting, explore the Tolmin Gorge, and take in the emerald-green waters of the Soča River.

Day 5: Kobarid
Drive to the historic town of Kobarid, home to a WWI museum. Hike to the Kozjak Waterfall and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Day 6-7: Lake Bohinj
Make your way to Lake Bohinj in the heart of Triglav National Park. Go swimming, take the cable car up Mount Vogel, or hike to the Savica Waterfall.

Day 8: Lake Bled
No Slovenian road trip is complete without a stop at Lake Bled. Row to the island, visit Bled Castle, and treat yourself to a slice of the famous cream cake.

Day 9: Škocjan or Postojna Caves
Explore Slovenia’s underground wonders. Škocjan Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site, while Postojna Cave offers a scenic ride through its vast chambers.

Day 10: Ljubljana
Finish your trip in Ljubljana. Slovenia’s capital is green, charming, and easy to explore. Stroll along the river, visit the castle, and enjoy a coffee on a sunny terrace.

Extra Tips for the Road

You can’t refill European gas bottles with a DIN connector in Slovenia. Instead, buy or exchange a local bottle. Don’t forget to bring the right adapter. Traveling in summer? Campsites around Bled and Bohinj are popular, book in advance. Ready to hit the road? Slovenia is waiting compact, green, and full of surprises. The perfect destination for your next camper adventure!

Italy



Off to Italy

Italy is a dream destination for campervan travelers. From rolling Tuscan hills to dramatic coastal roads and historic cities, this country has it all. Whether you're here for the food, the culture, or nature. Exploring Italy by camper van means freedom, charm, and adventure at every turn. Expect medieval towns, volcanic landscapes, and that irresistible dolce vita vibe.

Overnight Stays in Italy

Camping is well-established and popular in Italy. You'll find everything from basic camper spots to full-service campsites. Wild camping is officially not allowed, but in remote places it’s sometimes tolerated. Always check local regulations and use common sense.

Useful Info for Your Trip

Currency: Euro
Country Code: +39
Emergency Number: 112
Dutch Embassy: Via Michele Mercati 8, Rome
Tel: +39 06 322 86 01

Toll Roads and ZTL Zones

Highways in Italy are tolled using a ticket system. Be extra careful of ZTL zones (limited traffic zones) in cities, you’ll need a permit, and fines are enforced, even internationally.

Fuel and Charging Stations

Diesel (Gasolio) is widely available. LPG is called GPL and can be found in most regions. EV chargers are mostly in urban areas and along highways, less so in the south.

Driving in Italy

Driving in Italy can be exciting, especially in cities. Local drivers tend to be bold and expressive, so stay alert and confident.
Speed limits for campervans (≤ 3.5 tons):
- Urban areas: 50 km/h
- Rural roads: 90 km/h
- Highways: 130 km/h
Alcohol limit: 0.5‰ (0.0‰ for new drivers with less than 3 years of experience).

Bringing Pets

EU travel rules apply:
- European pet passport
- Microchip
- Rabies vaccination
Dogs are welcome at many campsites—and often in restaurants too.

Sample Itinerary: 14 Days Through Italy

Day 1-2: Lake Maggiore
Start in the north at peaceful Lake Maggiore. Visit the Borromean Islands and relax lakeside.

Day 3-4: Lake Como and Bergamo
Travel via Lake Como to charming Bergamo. Explore the old city (Città Alta) and try local polenta.

Day 5-6 Verona and Lake Garda
Walk the streets of Romeo and Juliet, then head to Lake Garda for swimming, cycling, and sipping drinks by the water.

Day 7-8: Venice
Park outside the city and explore Venice on foot or by vaporetto. Wander the alleys and soak up the beauty of Piazza San Marco.

Day 9-10: Tuscany (Chianti and San Gimignano)
Head to Tuscany’s wine country. Enjoy tastings in Chianti and visit the towers of medieval San Gimignano.

Day 11-12: Florence
Florence is an art and architecture haven. Admire the Duomo, Ponte Vecchio, and Michelangelo’s masterpieces. Campgrounds are just outside the city.

Day 13-14:Cinque Terre or Lucca
Wrap up your journey at the coast with a visit to Cinque Terre (train accessible from Levanto) or opt for laid-back Lucca with its walkable city walls.

Portugal



Off to Portugal

Portugal is the destination for adventurous travelers looking for sun-soaked days, turquoise seas, and idyllic beaches. Whether you're exploring the popular Algarve in the south or discovering the untouched nature near the Spanish border, this country offers endless opportunities for a camper van trip. Lisbon, with its rich culture and art, is of course a must-see along the way. So, pack up your camper, get ready for adventure, and discover everything Portugal has to offer!

Overnight Stays

Staying overnight in Portugal with your camper is a breeze. There are plenty of campsites and designated motorhome spots where you can enjoy a comfortable night. Wild camping? It's allowed, as long as you don’t stay longer than 48 hours in the same municipality (except in nature reserves, coastal areas, or where prohibited by signs).

Useful Information for Your Trip

Currency: Euro
Country Code: +351
Emergency Number: 112
Dutch Embassy: Avenida Infante Santo 43-5, 1399-011 Lisboa
Tel: +351 21 391 4900 (available 24/7)

Toll Roads and Payment

Most highways in Portugal require toll payments. For campervans under 3.5 tons, toll category 2 applies. There are two types of toll roads: the autostrada, where you pay at toll booths (by cash or credit card), and the electronic highways, where your camper is automatically registered. Make sure to register in advance for electronic toll payments to avoid fines! Easy payment options include Easytoll or a prepaid Tollcard, available at CTT points of sale.

Fuel and Charging Stations

Diesel (Gasóleo B7) is widely available, and there are over 340 LPG tank locations across the country. Electric vehicles can rely on a network of more than 3,000 charging stations, mainly near major cities and highways.

Driving in Portugal

Winter tires are not mandatory, but snow chains must be carried if road signs require them. Speed limits for campervans vary:
- Urban areas: 50 km/h
- Rural roads: 90 km/h
- Highways: 120 km/h (for campers ≤ 3.5 tons)
Just like at home, it is prohibited to drive with a blood alcohol level of 0.5 or higher. So enjoy the Portuguese wines, but drink responsibly!

Bringing Pets

In the European Union, there are exceptions for animals younger than three months. In Portugal, puppies do not need to be vaccinated against rabies as long as the mother is present, or if there is a declaration confirming that neither the young animal nor the mother has been exposed to potentially infected animals. For dogs, cats, and ferrets traveling within the EU, three general rules apply:
- They must have a European pet passport.
- They must be microchipped.
- They must be vaccinated against rabies.

Sample Itinerary: 10 days through Portugal

Day 1-2: Arrive in Lisbon
Arrive and settle into Portugal’s charming capital. Explore Alfama, Lisbon’s oldest district and ride the iconic tram 28 through the hills. Enjoy sunset views at Miradouro da Senhora do Monte. Stay the night at Orbitur Costa da Caparica, a location near the beach in Costa da Caparica, perfect for watching sunsets. Spend the day walking along the Tagus River and explore the Modern side of Lisbon. Stay another night at Costa da Caparica.

Day 3: Trip to Sinta
Travel to Sintra and explore the Palacio da Pena and Moorish Castle. Stay the night at Colares area, here are several rural and small camping options. They are often basic but located in peaceful, green surroundings. Perfect for when you want to take a walk into the mountains.

Day 4-5: Coimbra
Travel to Coimbra and explore the University of Coimbra. Enjoy dinner in the historic city center and stay the night at Quinta das Cegonhas where you can retreat into nature. Surrounded by mountains and forests, ideal for a longer countryside stay.

Day 6: Porto
Travel to porto and explore the charm and history of this city. Walk along the Douro River, its colorful buildings, steep hills, and iconic bridges create stunning views around every corner. The historic Ribeira district, with its narrow streets and tiled houses, is perfect for wandering. Stay the night at Parque de Campismo Salgueiros. Here you can enjoy the quiet beachside with quick access to porto. Great for combining beach and city.

Day 7-8: Algave
Travel to the Algarve and explore the cliffs and crystal-clear waters. It's a paradise for beach lovers and outdoor adventurers. Charming towns like Lagon and Tavira offer a blend of history and local culture. Stay the night at Orbitur Sagres just outside Sagres. Here you are surrounded by nature, great for surfers and hikers, because you are close to wild beaches and cliffs.

Day 9: Douro Valley
Travel to Douro Valley and visit vineyards and enjoy a long lunch with wine tasting. Stay the night at Quinta da Pacheca. Great when you like to stay at a working wine estate, surrounded by vineyards and enjoying local wine and foor.

Day 10: Return to Lisbon
Start you journey back to Lisbon, which will take about 5.5 hours. If time allows, stop along the way for one last scenic view of Portugal’s beautiful waters and breathtaking views. Arrive back in Lisbon for your last night and enjoy a dinner in the city.

Extra Tips for the Road

Gas bottles cannot be refilled in Portugal with a DIN connector, but you can purchase a local bottle from Repsol or Cepsa. Just make sure to have the correct adapters to connect it properly! Ready for your Portuguese adventure? Don’t forget to check your camper travel checklist to ensure you’re fully prepared! And remember: adventure is out there waiting to be discovered, and Portugal is the perfect destination for your camper trip!

Norway



Off to Norway

Norway is home to many breathtaking scenery, including calm forests, deep fjords, and high mountains. Even though the scenery is striking, there's a sense of peace that encourages you to take it easy and enjoy the experience. Whether you're hiking, driving through the countryside, or simply admiring the scenery, this is the type of area where you want to be outside.

Overnight stays

There are several choices for overnight accommodations for campervan users in Norway. The Right to Roam law permits wild camping for a maximum of two nights on uncultivated land as long as you are at least 150 meters away from buildings. However, it is prohibited to drive or park on such property without authorization. Official campsites are generally accessible and include facilities like Wi-Fi, power, and showers for added comfort. To help protect Norway's stunning landscapes, always abide by local laws, show consideration for the environment, and dispose of waste appropriately.

Useful information for your trip

Currency: Norwegian Krone (NOK)
Country Code: +47
Emergency Number: 112
Dutch Embassy in Norway: Oscars gate 29, 0352 Oslo, Norway
Phone Number: +47 23 33 36 00

Toll roads and vignettes

In Norway, there are no toll booths; instead, toll highways are controlled automatically. Instead, cameras scan your license plate, and costs are charged via systems like EPC for foreign vehicles and AutoPASS for local cars. To avoid additional costs, it's crucial to register with EPC within three days if you're importing your vehicle into Norway. In Norway, tolls are charged per use on designated roads, bridges, tunnels, and city zones; there is no vignette system. Reduced or free tolls are frequently useful for electric vehicles. Tolls are automatically billed because the majority of rental automobiles come with an AutoPASS tag.

Fuel and Charging stations

There are plenty of EV charging stations and gas stations throughout Norway. Diesel and gasoline (95/98) are widely available, and self-service outlets such as Circle K, Shell, and Uno-X are open around-the-clock. In rural areas, it's best to refuel early because rates are high compared to other countries and payment usually happens with a card. Additionally, Norway has one of the world's greatest networks for charging EVs. Normal AC chargers, 50 kW rapid DC chargers, and ultra-speed (150–350 kW) chargers are also available. Recharge, Mer, Ionity, and Tesla are some of the major suppliers. Although many stations now accept contactless cards, charging usually requires an app or RFID card.

Driving in Norway

If you have a valid license from your native country, you can drive in Norway for a maximum of three months.
- 50 km/h in cities.
- 80–90 km/h outside of towns.
- 100–110 km/h on highways.
Driving is done on the right side of the road.

Bringing pets

To bring your pet into Norway, you’ll need a pet passport with details on vaccinations, including a rabies vaccination (administered at least 21 days before travel). Pets must have a microchip for identification. Dogs also require a tapeworm treatment 1-5 days before entry (for non-EU countries). If traveling by car, you can enter via routes like the Swedish-Norwegian border, but your pet must still meet all health requirements. Make sure to check for any breed restrictions and possible fees for inspection or quarantine.

Sample Itinerary: 10 Days Across Norway

Day 1: Arrival in Oslo
Arrive in Oslo, the capital of Norway. Spend the day exploring the city: visit Vigeland Park and take a walk along the Aker Brygge waterfront. Stay overnight at the Ekeberg Camping. It’s just a 10 minute drive from downtown Oslo, offering stunning views of the city and the Oslofjord.

Day 2-3: Ormtjernet Lake
Drive from Oslo to Ormtjernet Lake, about a 3.5-hour drive. Set up camp and enjoy the peaceful surroundings. Take a relaxing walk along the lake, and enjoy the serene atmosphere, perfect for a quiet evening in nature. Stay overnight at a nearby camping spot. Spend the day hiking in the nearby forest areas. The region is known for its cross-country skiing in the winter, but it's equally beautiful in summer for peaceful walks and nature viewing. After hiking, relax by the lake, reading or just enjoying the silence of nature. Camp again by the lake.

Day 4-5: Bygdin Lake
Drive to Bygdin Lake, a stunning spot located about 2.5 hours from Ormtjernet. Upon arrival, enjoy a nature hike around the lake, surrounded by majestic mountains. It’s a perfect place for hikers and nature lovers. Find a quiet spot to camp or stay at a nearby mountain lodge. Enjoy an evening walk around the lake, watching the sunset reflect off the water. Explore the mountain trails surrounding Bygdin Lake. The area is great for photography, hiking, and appreciating Norway's untouched beauty. Consider taking a boat ride on the lake to get a different perspective of the mountains. Camp for another night near the lake.

Day 6-7: Kristiansand Feriesenter
Drive south to Kristiansand Feriesenter, which takes about 5.5 hours. Arrive at the campsite, located close to a sandy beach. The area is quiet, perfect for walking along the shore. Take a walk during sunset and enjoy the calmness of the beach with no crowds around. Relax by the water, and enjoy the natural beauty. Stay overnight at the campsite. Spend the day enjoying the peaceful beach. You can take a long stroll along the shore, read, or just relax. If you’re interested, explore nearby trails or simply enjoy the solitude of the campsite. Enjoy a quiet evening by the beach.

Day 8-9: Olden
Drive to Olden, which takes about 6.5 hours from Kristiansand. Upon arrival, set up camp near Oldevatnet Lake, surrounded by towering mountains, glaciers, and waterfalls. The views here are truly magical, with the calm lake reflecting the high peaks and glaciers. Enjoy a peaceful evening by the water. Spend the day exploring Olden and its breathtaking surroundings. You can easily rent a rowboat and enjoy a peaceful paddle on the lake, taking in the stunning scenery. For the adventurous, hike into the nearby forest or visit nearby Briksdal Glacier. Enjoy the natural beauty, calm waters, and towering mountains as you relax by the lake. Camp by the lake for the night.

Day 10: Return to Oslo
Start your journey back to Oslo, which will take about 7.5 hours. If time allows, stop along the way for one last scenic view of the fjords or mountain landscapes. Arrive back in Oslo for your last night, and enjoy a farewell dinner in the city.

Extra Tips for the Road

- Watch Out for Wildlife
- Prepare for Midnight Sun and Polar Nights
Ready to hit the road? Norway is waiting for you with it’s green landscapes, mountains and all the other surprises Norway brings. The perfect destination for your next camper adventure!
